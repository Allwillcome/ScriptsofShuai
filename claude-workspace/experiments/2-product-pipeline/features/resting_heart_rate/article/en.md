## What Is Resting Heart Rate?
Resting Heart Rate (RHR) refers to the number of heartbeats per minute when you are completely relaxed and not engaged in any physical activity. It is an important indicator for assessing cardiovascular health and overall fitness level.

## Why Is Resting Heart Rate Important?
Resting heart rate reflects how efficiently your heart works under minimal load. A lower resting heart rate typically indicates better cardiovascular health and higher fitness levels, showing that the heart can provide adequate blood flow to the body at a lower heart rate. People who exercise regularly usually have a lower resting heart rate because their heart muscle is stronger and more efficient.

## Normal Resting Heart Rate Range
For most adults, the normal resting heart rate range is 60 to 100 beats per minute. However, this range may vary due to individual differences. Athletes and people who exercise regularly may have a lower resting heart rate, sometimes even below 60 beats per minute.

## Factors Affecting Resting Heart Rate
Several factors can affect resting heart rate, including:
- **Age**: Resting heart rate typically increases with age.
- **Fitness Level**: Regular physical activity usually lowers resting heart rate.
- **Temperature**: Higher temperatures and humidity levels can increase resting heart rate.
- **Body Position**: Resting heart rate varies depending on whether you are standing, sitting, or lying down.
- **Emotions**: Stress, anxiety, and excitement can elevate resting heart rate.
- **Medication**: Some medications can affect resting heart rate.

## Monitoring Resting Heart Rate
Regularly monitoring your resting heart rate can provide valuable insights into cardiovascular health. Tracking changes over time can help identify trends and potential health issues. For example, an abnormally high resting heart rate may indicate overtraining, stress, or illness, while a consistently declining resting heart rate may indicate improved fitness levels.



## Why Does It Differ from Apple Health?
You may notice differences between PeakWatch's calculated resting heart rate and the values in Apple Health, due to different data filtering criteria:
- Apple Health: Its resting heart rate is more like a quiet heart rate. It may include low heart rate data during the day when sedentary, working at a desk, or resting. This data is susceptible to interference from caffeine, emotions, or environmental noise, causing fluctuations.
- PeakWatch: Focuses on athletic performance and recovery. To exclude daytime interference, we adopt a stricter "nighttime priority" strategy, aiming to capture the body's purest baseline state.

## PeakWatch's Calculation Mechanism
To provide meaningful recovery data, PeakWatch uses a dynamic algorithm based on sleep physiology:
- Nighttime Sleep Priority (Gold Standard): If the system detects that you wore the watch to sleep, it will prioritize calculating the average heart rate during the first 4 hours after falling asleep. Scientific research shows that slow-wave sleep (deep sleep) is mostly concentrated in the first half of the night, when heart rate is most stable and best reflects the body's true recovery from the previous day's training load.
- Data Completion: When nighttime sleep data is missing, the system automatically falls back to using existing resting heart rate data to ensure continuity of trend records.


## Conclusion
Resting heart rate is a simple yet powerful measure of heart health and fitness level. By understanding and tracking your resting heart rate, you can make informed decisions about your physical activity, stress management, and overall health.
